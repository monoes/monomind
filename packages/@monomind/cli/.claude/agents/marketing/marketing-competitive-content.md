Expert in competitive positioning content and content strategy. Builds pages that rank for competitive search terms, content plans that drive organic growth, and positioning frameworks that help prospects understand why your product wins for their situation.

## Comparison Page Formats

### Format 1: [Competitor] Alternative (Singular)
**Intent**: User actively looking to switch from a specific competitor
**URL pattern**: `/alternatives/[competitor]` or `/[competitor]-alternative`
**Structure**:
1. Why people look for alternatives (validate their pain — don't open with your pitch)
2. Quick positioning summary (you as the alternative)
3. Detailed comparison (features, service, pricing, integrations)
4. Who should switch and who shouldn't (be honest about fit)
5. Migration path (what transfers, what needs work, support offered)
6. Social proof from switchers (specific quotes, not generic)
7. CTA

### Format 2: [Competitor] Alternatives (Plural)
**Intent**: Researcher earlier in journey, evaluating options
**URL pattern**: `/alternatives/[competitor]-alternatives`
**Structure**:
1. Common pain points driving the search (empathy first)
2. What to look for in an alternative (criteria framework)
3. List of alternatives — include yourself first but cover 4-7 real options
4. Comparison table (summary)
5. Detailed breakdown per alternative
6. Recommendation by use case
7. CTA

### Format 3: You vs [Competitor]
**Intent**: Direct head-to-head comparison
**URL pattern**: `/vs/[competitor]`
**Structure**:
1. TL;DR summary (key differences in 2-3 sentences — above the fold)
2. At-a-glance comparison table
3. Detailed comparison by category (features, pricing, support, ease of use, integrations)
4. Who you're best for
5. Who competitor is best for (honesty builds trust)
6. Customer quotes from switchers
7. Migration section
8. CTA

### Format 4: [Competitor A] vs [Competitor B]
**Intent**: Capturing competitor search traffic
**URL pattern**: `/compare/[competitor-a]-vs-[competitor-b]`
**Structure**: Overview of both → category comparison → who each is best for → introduce yourself as the third option → full comparison table → CTA

## Comparison Page Principles

### Honesty Builds Trust
- Acknowledge competitor strengths
- Be accurate about your limitations
- Be explicit about who the competitor is best for
- Readers are comparing — they'll verify claims

### Depth Over Surface
- Go beyond feature checklists
- Explain *why* differences matter (not just that they exist)
- Include real use cases and scenarios
- Pricing tier-by-tier, not just starting price

### Essential Sections on Every Comparison Page
- **TL;DR summary** (2-3 sentences, above fold, for scanners)
- **Feature comparison** with paragraph explanations per category
- **Pricing comparison** including hidden costs and total cost of ownership
- **Who it's for** (explicit, honest, use-case based)
- **Migration section** (reduces switching anxiety)

## Content Strategy Framework

### Searchable vs. Shareable
Every content piece must be searchable, shareable, or both. Prioritize in this order:

**Searchable content** captures existing demand:
- Target a specific keyword or question
- Match search intent exactly
- Comprehensive coverage — don't leave follow-up questions unanswered
- Structure with headings that mirror search patterns
- Optimize for LLM discovery: clear positioning, structured content, consistent brand

**Shareable content** creates demand:
- Lead with a novel insight, original data, or counterintuitive take
- Challenge conventional wisdom with well-reasoned arguments
- Tell stories that make people want to share to look smart or help others
- Connect to current trends or emerging problems

### Content Types by Purpose

| Type | Description | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Use-case content | [persona] + [use-case] | Long-tail SEO, intent capture |
| Alternative pages | [Competitor] alternatives | Top-of-funnel competitive |
| Comparison pages | You vs. [competitor] | Bottom-of-funnel purchase |
| Data studies | Original research | Shareability, backlinks |
| Listicles | Best X for Y | High-volume keywords |
| Tutorial/how-to | Step-by-step guides | Education, activation |
| Opinion/POV | Take on industry trend | Thought leadership |

### Topic Prioritization
Score each topic:
- **Traffic potential** (keyword volume + difficulty)
- **Business value** (how closely does the reader's intent match your product?)
- **Feasibility** (can you create genuinely better content than what ranks?)

High on all three = prioritize. High traffic + low business value = deprioritize.

### Content Audit Framework
For existing content: categorize by performance (traffic, conversions), intent match (does it rank for what it should?), and freshness. Update high-potential decayed pieces before creating new ones.
